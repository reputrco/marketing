# Reputr Product Foundation

Reputr is a review management platform for businesses that earn trust through real customer experiences. It helps a business collect feedback at the moment the experience is still fresh, keep private feedback inside its own dashboard, and guide satisfied customers toward the public review destination the business has configured.

At its simplest, Reputr turns a normal customer interaction into a repeatable review flow. A customer scans a QR code, opens a branded review page, chooses a star rating, and leaves feedback without downloading an app or creating an account. Reputr captures that response first. If the rating meets the business's chosen threshold, the customer can be invited to continue to a public review destination such as a Google review link, Google Business Profile, or another public URL. If the rating is below the threshold, the feedback stays private so the business can understand the issue and follow up.

The product exists because most businesses do not have a review-quality problem. They have a timing and process problem. Happy customers often leave before writing anything publicly. Staff forget to ask at the right moment. Review links get buried in messages. Meanwhile, unhappy customers are often more motivated to post on their own. Reputr gives the business a simple, consistent process that works in the physical places where customers already interact with the brand.

## Product Narrative

Reputr starts from a practical belief: customers already have opinions, but most businesses do not capture those opinions at the right time. The best moment to ask is not days later in a forgotten message. It is while the customer is still at the counter, table, checkout, reception desk, receipt, product package, service point, or follow-up touchpoint.

The product gives each business a hosted review page with its own name, URL, branding, colors, logo, welcome message, and feedback settings. The business can generate QR codes that point to that page and place them where customers naturally pause. A restaurant can put the QR on a table card or receipt. A salon can place it at checkout. A clinic can use it near reception. A repair shop can add it to a printed handout. A retail store can put it on packaging or signage. The same basic flow can work across many local, service, and multi-location businesses because the behavior is simple: scan, rate, submit.

For customers, Reputr is intentionally lightweight. The page opens in a mobile browser, asks one clear question, and collects the right amount of feedback. Customers can leave a rating quickly, add written feedback when the business enables it, and answer optional custom questions when those are relevant. The experience is meant to feel like part of the business, not like a generic third-party form.

For owners and teams, Reputr becomes a private feedback and review-growth workspace. Every response lands in the dashboard. The business can see ratings, comments, sources, trends, conversion, and follow-up needs. The product is not only asking for reviews; it is helping the team build a repeatable habit around feedback, service recovery, and reputation growth.

## The Experience Reputr Creates

A business begins by setting up its first review page. It adds its business name, category, Google place information, review page URL, and basic contact details. The business can keep the page active when it wants to collect reviews or make it inactive when the review page should no longer accept submissions.

Once the business exists, Reputr gives it QR codes for different customer touchpoints. The current QR source types include counter, receipt, product label, and ads. These source-specific QR codes help the business understand where review activity is coming from instead of treating every scan as the same. The business can customize QR colors, choose the right size, add the business logo when available, copy the review link, and download QR files for print or digital use. Paid businesses can also create branded, print-ready poster assets for QR placement.

When a customer scans the QR code, the review page opens immediately. The page can show the business logo, brand colors, a welcome message, star rating, optional written feedback, and custom fields. The page only shows additional fields that are enabled and relevant to the selected rating. This keeps the experience short while still letting the business collect richer context when needed.

After submission, Reputr stores the feedback privately inside the business dashboard. The next step depends on the rating threshold. A common setup is to treat 4 stars and above as the public-review path. In that path, the customer can be sent to the configured public review destination after a thank-you screen or immediately, depending on the business setting. For lower ratings, the customer sees a private thank-you message and can be asked optional follow-up questions so the team can learn what happened.

This creates a cleaner flow for everyone. Happy customers get a clear path to share their experience publicly. Unhappy customers are acknowledged privately before their frustration becomes a public reputation issue. The business hears from both groups instead of only seeing the extremes that make it to public review sites.

## Owner Dashboard

The dashboard is the operating center for each business. It gives owners and teams a way to manage review collection, page design, QR assets, feedback, analytics, notifications, and subscription status from one place.

The review page customization area lets the business control how the public page feels. Owners can set the rating threshold, choose the redirect destination, control redirect timing, decide whether to show a thank-you screen, customize brand colors, upload or hide the business image, edit the welcome message, choose whether written feedback is shown or required, change the submit button text, and write different thank-you messages for high and low ratings.

The same area also supports custom questions. A business can add up to five additional fields to the review page, such as short answers, longer comments, email, phone, dropdown choices, or simple checkboxes. Fields can be required or optional, enabled or disabled, ordered, and shown only for specific ratings. This is useful when the business wants feedback to include helpful context, such as staff member, service type, order type, visit reason, or permission-style details.

For below-threshold experiences, Reputr supports follow-up fields. These are separate from the main review form and are designed for service recovery. A business can add a follow-up message, choose the button text, choose the submit text, and add up to three follow-up fields. This gives the customer a simple way to share details after a low rating, and it gives the team more context before reaching out.

The reviews area shows submitted feedback with rating, comment, source, date, custom field details, and follow-up information. Owners can filter by date, rating, source, and search terms. They can see positive and negative counts, average rating, total reviews, and which feedback needs attention. Follow-up statuses can be managed as open, in progress, responded, or closed so private feedback becomes a workflow instead of a passive inbox.

The analytics area turns review collection into measurable activity. It shows review performance, positive rate, five-star share, negative rate, average rating trend, page visits, reviews submitted, redirects, conversion rate, rating distribution, source distribution, rating trends, and funnel trends. This helps a business answer practical questions: Are people scanning? Are they submitting? Which placements work? Are ratings improving? How many submitted customers continue to the public destination?

Business settings cover the practical details around the business profile and review destination. Owners can edit business name, category, phone, email, logo, Google place details, street address, city, country, website, custom public review destination, and active status. They can also enable or disable low-rating email alerts and weekly review summary emails.

Multi-business support is a core part of the product. A user can manage more than one business from the same dashboard. Each business gets its own review page, QR assets, settings, analytics, subscription state, and collected feedback. This makes Reputr useful for owners with multiple locations, agencies or operators managing several local brands, and growing businesses that want each location to keep its own reputation signals.

## Customer Fit

Reputr fits businesses where trust is earned through direct customer experience and where online confidence affects calls, bookings, walk-ins, purchases, or repeat visits.

The product is especially natural for restaurants, cafes, bars, bakeries, salons, barbers, spas, wellness studios, gyms, clinics, dental offices, veterinary and pet services, senior care, childcare, tutoring, retail stores, grocery and convenience stores, auto services, repair shops, home services, cleaning services, contractors, landscaping, moving and storage, hotels, event venues, entertainment businesses, real estate offices, property management, professional services, financial services, legal services, creative services, and other local or service-led businesses.

The common thread is not the industry label. The common thread is the need to turn real customer moments into visible trust. If a business depends on people choosing it based on reputation, recent experiences, ratings, local search confidence, or referrals, Reputr can support the review process.

The strongest customer is usually a business that already delivers good experiences but lacks a consistent system for asking, capturing, routing, and learning from feedback. Reputr is less about persuading customers to say something artificial and more about making it easy for real customers to share what already happened.

## Why Reputr Feels Different

Most review requests are either too passive or too blunt. A business may rely on staff to ask verbally, send generic links, print a static review URL, or direct every customer straight to a public site. Those methods create friction for happy customers and risk exposing unresolved dissatisfaction publicly.

Reputr adds a first-party feedback step before the public review destination. That one step changes the experience. The business hears from every customer who submits, not only from people who complete a public review. It can learn from private feedback, identify issues early, and still give satisfied customers a clear path to public proof.

The product is also built around physical customer moments. QR codes are not an afterthought; they are the bridge between the offline experience and the online reputation system. Source-specific QR codes make placement measurable, and downloadable assets make the system usable in real spaces.

Reputr is owner-friendly rather than enterprise-heavy. The dashboard is meant for busy operators who want clear actions and simple signals: collect more feedback, see what is working, follow up on issues, and keep review collection running every day. It avoids making the customer create an account, avoids long forms by default, and gives the business practical controls without forcing a complex setup.

The private-first model also makes the product useful even before public review volume grows. A business can start learning from feedback immediately. It can spot repeated issues, compare locations or sources, and build confidence that the review request process is happening consistently.

Paid plans are positioned per business, with more advanced customization, all QR source types, full branding, custom questions, alerts, summaries, analytics, and spreadsheet-ready export access.

The commercial promise should stay simple: start with one business, generate a QR code, test the customer experience, and decide after the trial whether Reputr should become part of the daily review process.

## Messaging Principles

Reputr should be described as a product that captures customer feedback first and grows public reputation second. The strongest language is practical, owner-friendly, and grounded in real moments: counters, receipts, tables, product labels, posters, ads, front desks, checkout points, and service handoffs.

The product should not be framed as a way to fake reviews, hide legitimate criticism, guarantee Google rankings, guarantee review volume, or manipulate public platforms. It should be framed as a clearer feedback flow that helps a business ask at the right time, learn privately, and guide satisfied customers toward the public review destination the business has chosen.

Good Reputr copy should make the product feel easy to start, easy for customers to complete, and useful for daily operations. It should avoid sounding like a generic reputation agency, a complicated customer experience platform, or an abstract analytics tool. The product is concrete: scan, rate, route, learn, follow up, and improve.

The most useful repeated ideas are:

- Capture feedback while the experience is still fresh.
- Make review collection a repeatable habit instead of a staff memory task.
- Give happy customers a clear path to public proof.
- Keep private feedback private so teams can act early.
- See which QR placements, sources, and locations are working.
- Give every business or location its own branded review flow.
- Let customers submit quickly without an app or login.

## Reusable Product Summary

Reputr helps local and multi-business operators collect customer feedback through branded QR review pages. Customers scan a QR code, rate their experience, and submit feedback in seconds without an app or login. Reputr captures every response privately first, then routes customers based on the business's rating threshold. Satisfied customers can continue to the configured public review destination, while lower ratings stay inside Reputr with optional follow-up fields so the team can understand what happened and respond.

The dashboard gives owners the tools to customize the review page, generate source-specific QR codes, manage reviews, track analytics, export data, receive alerts and summaries, and run the same process across multiple businesses. Reputr is built for businesses that depend on local trust, repeat visits, online confidence, and real customer proof.
